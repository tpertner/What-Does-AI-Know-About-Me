### Step 2: The Composite Profile Prompt

Take your compiled data map and paste it into ANY AI assistant with this prompt:

```
I've conducted a data transparency audit across 4 AI platforms. Below is what each platform knows about me. I want you to:

1. BUILD THE COMPOSITE
Combine all of this into a single, unified profile of me — as if you were a data broker assembling a dossier from multiple sources. What does the FULL picture look like when you put it all together?

2. IDENTIFY THE OVERLAPS
Where do multiple platforms agree about me? These high-confidence data points are the most "locked in" parts of my digital identity across AI.

3. FIND THE GAPS
What does one platform know that others don't? These gaps reveal which platform I've been most open with — and which has the most unique leverage on my data.

4. ASSESS THE RISK
If ALL of this data were compiled by a single entity (a data broker, an attacker, or a future employer):
- What could they confidently conclude about me?
- What decisions could they make about me based on this profile?
- What's the most damaging combination of data points across platforms?

5. GRADE MY EXPOSURE
On a scale of 1-10 (1 = minimal footprint, 10 = comprehensive digital profile), rate my overall AI data exposure. Explain your rating.

[PASTE YOUR COMPILED DATA MAP HERE]
```

## Key Insights to Look For

### The Fragmentation Problem
Most people use different AI tools for different tasks. The result: no single platform has the full picture, but *collectively* they hold a comprehensive profile. Ask yourself — if someone merged all of these, what would they know?

### The Memory Asymmetry
ChatGPT and Claude actively remember things about you. Gemini pulls from your broader Google life. Perplexity tracks your curiosity. Each creates a different *kind* of profile — behavioral vs. declarative vs. inferential.

### The Training Question
Your conversations may be training the next version of the model. Even if your specific data is "anonymized," the patterns, preferences, and linguistic fingerprints you leave behind become part of the model's general knowledge.

### The Deletion Illusion
Deleting a conversation doesn't always delete:
- Memories derived from that conversation (ChatGPT, Claude)
- Training data already ingested
- Backup copies and logs
- Metadata (timestamps, token counts, session data)
- Flagged content retained for safety review
